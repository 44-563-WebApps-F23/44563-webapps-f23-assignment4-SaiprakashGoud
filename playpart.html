An Enemy of the People Act 2 by Ibsen

(ASLAKSEN appears at the door. He is poorly but decently dressed, in black, with a slightly crumpled white neckcloth; he wears gloves and has a felt hat in his hand.) Aslaksen (bowing). Excuse my taking the liberty, Doctor— Dr. Stockmann (getting up). Ah, it is you, Aslaksen! Aslaksen. Yes, Doctor. Hovstad (standing up). Is it me you want, Aslaksen? Aslaksen. No; I didn't know I should find you here. No, it was the Doctor I— Dr. Stockmann. I am quite at your service. What is it? Aslaksen. Is what I heard from Mr. Billing true, sir—that you mean to improve our water supply? Dr. Stockmann. Yes, for the Baths.
